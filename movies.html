if you’re chasing that Lil Peep vibe, you’re probably looking for movies that feel emo, melancholic, romantic, gritty, and dreamlike, with themes of youth, heartbreak, drugs, and beauty in sadness. Here are 3 movies that capture that aesthetic:

1- Kids (1995):

- Title: Kids

- Year: 1995

- Director: Larry Clark

- Writer: Harmony Korine

- Setting: New York City, mid-'90s

Kids follows a single chaotic day in the lives of a group of New York City teenagers drifting through sex, drugs, and aimless rebellion.
At the center is Telly, a 17-year-old whose obsession is to sleep with as many virgins as possible — without ever using protection. Meanwhile, Jennie, one of his past partners, learns she’s HIV-positive and sets out to find him before he infects anyone else.
As Telly and his friend Casper wander the city, skating, stealing, and getting high, the film exposes the raw energy and emptiness of youth culture — all set against the backdrop of 1990s urban decay.

2- Requiem for a dream:

- Title: Requiem for a Dream

- Year: 2000

- Director: Darren Aronofsky

- Screenplay: Hubert Selby Jr. & Darren Aronofsky

- Setting: Brooklyn, New York City, late '90s

Requiem for a Dream follows four people in Brooklyn whose lives spiral into chaos due to different forms of addiction. Harry, his girlfriend Marion, and his friend Tyrone become trapped by heroin, while Harry’s mother Sara becomes addicted to amphetamine diet pills in her obsessive dream of appearing on television. Each character’s pursuit of happiness turns into a nightmare, ending in physical and emotional ruin.

3- Girl, Interrupted:

- Title: Girl, Interrupted

- Year: 1999

- Director: James Mangold

- Screenplay: James Mangold & Lisa Loomter

- Setting: Claymoore Psychiatric Hospital, late '60s

Girl, Interrupted follows Susanna Kaysen, an 18-year-old woman who is admitted to a psychiatric hospital in the late 1960s after a suicide attempt and being diagnosed with borderline personality disorder. At Claymoore Psychiatric Hospital, she encounters a group of young women struggling with various mental illnesses, As Susanna forms connections with these women, particularly Lisa, she begins to question the nature of sanity, the meaning of normalcy, and whether her place in society truly makes her “crazy.” Her time in Claymoore becomes a journey of self-discovery, healing, and confrontation with truth.
